Schedule of Events

Friday, January 30 Events

The first official evening of the Richmond Meltdown Winter Ice Festival has plenty to offer for everyone!

Paint and Take Souvenirs

Paint your own souvenir and take it home with you offered by Paint the Towne from 10 am – 9 pm! Only one souvenir per child. Available on a “First Come, First Serve Basis."

The Meltdown Warmup at the Grassroots Center

Grassroots Action Resource Center will host the Meltdown Warmup with FREE coffee and hot chocolate, children’s activities, a selfie station, and more! Stop by anytime between 4 – 9 pm!

Veach’s Game Day

Get warmed up and play some favorite classic games with your family at this free event sponsored by Veach’s Toy Station from 5 – 7 pm!

Celebrity Chili Cook-off: Fill Your Bowl, Feed Your Soul

Help us heat up the Meltdown in a fun and delicious way to warm up and sample the best chili and homemade breads served by local celebrity “chefs” while supporting your favorite charities. Join us at Morrisson-Reeves Library from 5 – 7 pm. Proceeds help fund non-profits in Richmond and The United Way of the Whitewater Valley.

Ice Fights

Watch in awe as these fighters carve through 600 pounds of ice with chainsaws! The action starts at 7:30 pm at the 7th Street Park!

Saturday, January 31 Events

We make sure to jam pack Saturday with free events for everyone!

Freeze Your Buns Off Fun Run

Join us for a brisk morning workout as we run through the Richmond Meltdown! Interested runners will meet in 7th Street Park at 7:30 am for a run of your choice! Choose to take the fun run at your own pace, running the mileage of your choice!

Meet the Ice Carvers at Breakfast

You saw them in action on Friday night, now is the opportunity to meet them in person. The Ice Fighters will be filling their plates and bellies at our Meet and Greet breakfast from 8 – 11 am! The American Legion Post 65 (109 North 6th Street) will be cooking up a tasty meal for only $5.

The Meltdown Warmup at the Grassroots Center

Grassroots Action Resource Center will host the Meltdown Warmup with FREE coffee and hot chocolate, children’s activities, a selfie station, and more! Stop by anytime between 10 am – 9 pm!

Paint and Take Souvenirs

Paint your own souvenir and take it home with you offered by Paint the Towne from 10 am – 7 pm! Only one souvenir per child. Available on a “First Come, First Serve Basis.”

Frozen Fairy Trail

Stop by the Welcome Center between 10 am – 4 pm to participate in frozen fairy crafts, enjoy free hot chocolate, peek into our fairy door and much more! Pick up the Frozen Fairy Trail Brochure and travel to other participating fairy gardens, houses and doors throughout the Meltdown Festival for even more crafts, activities and free fun!

Veach’s Game Day

Get warmed up and play some favorite classic games with your family at this free event sponsored by Veach’s Toy Station from 10 am – 12 pm!

Blue Heron Singers

Attend a lively practice of the Blue Heron Singers, a Native American Pow Wow Drum Group, at the Tin Lizzie Café from 10 am – 12 pm and 1:30 – 3 pm!

Live Music Festival

The Bard Room at Morrisson-Reeves Library will come alive with a musical performances from 1:30 – 4 pm! Main performer Keith Lykins is a guitarist like you’ve never heard before, and will perform a compositional style termed “Urban Tribal Music” or “Slap Guitar.”

Craft Party and Book Launch with Chuck Avery

Local author Chuck Avery will be reading and signing his new book, “A Reason To Be.” While visiting, enjoy a craft! It’s all in the Children’s Department at Morrisson-Reeves Library from 2 – 4 pm!

Veach’s Game Day

Get warmed up and play some favorite classic games with your family at this free event sponsored by Veach’s Toy Station from 4 – 6 pm!

Awards and Crowning Ceremony

Come congratulate the winners of the Coloring Contest and Snowman Building Contest! We’ll also announce the Meltdown Court from our Jack Frost/Ice Queen and Jack Frost Jr./Ice Princess contest. This ceremony will take place at 7th Street Park at 6:30 pm.

Ice Fights

Watch in awe as these fighters carve through 600 pounds of ice with chainsaws! The action starts at 7:30 pm at the 7th Street Park!
